Waltham High School

City of Waltham

service: XGD

market: K-12

timeline: 2020–2024

The design for the new Waltham High School balances academic rigor with hands-on learning through a full integration of Career and Technical Education (CTE) spaces. The Experiential Graphics and Signage sought to represent the growing diversity of the city while also celebrating the Waltham’s rich history as an industrial pioneer. The tile murals, vinyl graphics, nearly 100 posters that fill the school's halls weave together stories curated to reinforce the values found in what the students, staff, and community defined as the portrait of a Waltham student.


Diversity was brought to life both through the use of patterns and symbology, as well as through curating stories of diverse individuals with connections to the city. All of that content was then distributed to align with the programming of the school, and color was used strategically to reinforce wayfinding and placemaking in shared spaces. In addition to research and developing the visual concept, I was also deeply involved with the execution of every piece of the project, from detailed design through construction administration and photography.
